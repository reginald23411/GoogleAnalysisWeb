    • Spaghetti Bolognese


    It doesn't take a lot of ingredients (or a lot of money) to make a classic Bolognese recipe. What it does take, though, is patience for the sauce to achieve the ideal authentic texture. You’re going to go low and slow-it'll take around 3 hours, but most of that cook time is hands-off.

    Prep time

    15 min.

    Cook Time

    45 min.

    Portions

    5

    Instruction

    Step 1

    Finely chop onion, celery, and carrot using a food processor. Set aside.

    Step 2

    In a large pot, cook beef clumps over medium heat with a pinch of salt until lightly browned. Transfer to a bowl.

    Step 3

    In the same pot, cook pancetta until crispy. Add the chopped vegetables and cook until soft.

    Step 4

    Add the beef back to the pot, pour in the wine, and cook over medium-low heat. Press down on the beef with a wooden spoon until finely ground and the wine evaporates.

    Step 5

    Stir in tomato paste, bay leaf, and nutmeg. Cook until the tomato paste darkens slightly.

    Step 6

    In a separate pot, cook pasta in boiling salted water until al dente. Fresh pasta takes about 3 minutes, while dry pasta cooks for about 2 minutes less than package directions.

    Step 7

    Use tongs to transfer the pasta to the pot with the sauce. Add 1 cup of pasta cooking liquid and ½ cup of Parmesan. Simmer and toss until the pasta is well-coated and the liquid has slightly thickened.

    Step 8

    Serve the pasta on a platter, topped with more Parmesan. Enjoy!


    Ingredient

    1 medium onion, chopped

    1 celery stalk, chopped

    1 small carrot, peeled, chopped

    3 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil

    1 lb. ground beef chuck (20% fat), patted dry

    3 oz. thinly sliced pancetta, finely chopped

    ¼ cup tomato paste


    Utensil

    knife

    oven

    spatula

    chopper
